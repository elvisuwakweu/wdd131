About The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (often informally called the LDS Church) is a Christian restorationist church that considers itself the restoration of the original church founded by Jesus Christ. Founded in 1830 by Joseph Smith in New York, it has millions of members worldwide.

Temples

Temples are sacred houses of the Lord where members participate in essential ordinances. Some well-known temples include:

  • Kirtland Ohio Temple - Dedicated in 1836, one of the earliest temples in Church history.
  • Salt Lake City Utah Temple - Dedicated in 1893, iconic and central to Church headquarters.
  • Los Angeles California Temple - Dedicated in 1956, serves members of Southern California.
  • Paris France Temple - Dedicated in 2017, welcoming members from all of France and nearby regions.

Mission and Beliefs

The Church emphasizes faith in Jesus Christ, family values, community service, and missionary work. Members are encouraged to live morally upright lives, study scriptures, and participate in regular worship.
